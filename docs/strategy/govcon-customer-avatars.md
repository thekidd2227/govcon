# GovCon Customer Avatars

Detailed buyer personas for ARCG Systems (Ariel's River Contracting Group LLC) GovCon marketing. Each avatar drives content pillar selection, hook tone, CTA mapping, and offer construction across LinkedIn, Facebook, and Instagram.

ARCG positioning: certified SDVOSB execution partner for facility support and operational services. Primary wedge is micro-purchases, simplified acquisitions, state/local set-asides, and prime subcontracting under a $250K ceiling, with a 24–72 hour response posture and a 25% minimum net margin.

---

## Avatar 1 — Federal Contracting Officer (CO / CS)

- **Role / title:** Contracting Officer (1102 series), Contract Specialist, Purchase Card Holder, Small Business Specialist
- **Agency or organization type:** Federal civilian agencies (GSA, VA, USDA, DOI, DHS components), DoD installations, federal field offices, regional procurement centers
- **Buying authority and likely threshold:**
  - Micro-purchase authority (under $10K) — purchase card, immediate decision
  - Simplified acquisition (up to $250K) — sole-source allowed for SDVOSB / 8(a) / HUBZone
  - Above $250K — typically out of scope for ARCG unless teaming with a prime
- **Primary pain points:**
  - Year-end and quarter-end obligation pressure with shrinking vendor lists
  - SAM.gov registrations expiring, vendors not responding inside 72 hours
  - Set-aside quotas (SDVOSB 3%, small business 23%) that need real, responsive vendors
  - Documentation churn — capability statements that don't match NAICS, missing reps & certs
  - Burned by vendors who win awards then can't mobilize
- **What they need to believe before contacting ARCG:**
  - ARCG is registered in SAM.gov, active, and current on reps & certs
  - The right NAICS (561710 + secondary lanes) is covered
  - ARCG actually answers the phone and replies inside 72 hours
  - The certifications (SDVOSB, MBE, SBE, DBE, HUBZone) are documented and verifiable
  - ARCG can perform inside their threshold without sub drama
- **Proof / trust signals that matter:**
  - SAM.gov UEI visible on capability statement
  - SDVOSB certification displayed correctly (SBA VetCert reference, not "self-certified" language)
  - Clean, single-page capability statement with NAICS, PSC, DUNS/UEI, POC
  - Past performance phrased as "facility support services delivered" — not invented federal awards
  - Plain, procurement-aware language; no marketing fluff
- **Objections:**
  - "I've never heard of you" — handled with capability statement + cert proof
  - "Can you really respond in 72 hours?" — handled with stated response SLA and POC
  - "Do you have past performance on this NAICS?" — handled with honest scope of completed work, including commercial and state/local
  - "Will you actually self-perform or sub everything?" — handled with prime-managed, sub-supported language
- **Preferred outreach angle:**
  - Procurement-aware, documentation-first, zero hype
  - "Vendor ready for micro-purchase and simplified acquisition under $250K"
  - LinkedIn DM + capability statement attached, never a cold pitch deck
- **Best content topics:**
  - SDVOSB sole-source under SAT explained
  - Micro-purchase vendor readiness checklist
  - How a clean capability statement saves a CO 20 minutes
  - End-of-fiscal-year obligation patterns and how a responsive vendor helps
  - NAICS 561710 set-aside opportunities
- **CTA that fits the buyer:**
  - "Download our capability statement"
  - "Request a 15-minute capabilities call"
  - "Add ARCG to your vendor list"
- **Offers most relevant to them:**
  - Capability statement PDF
  - Sole-source justification language pre-drafted
  - Documented response SLA (24–72 hours)
  - Pest control, janitorial, translation, staffing, emergency response — bundled or single lane

## Avatar 2 — State / Local Procurement Lead

- **Role / title:** Procurement Manager, Purchasing Agent, MWBE Coordinator, City/County Buyer, School District Purchasing Director
- **Agency or organization type:** State agencies, county governments, municipal governments, school districts, public housing authorities, transit authorities, public health departments
- **Buying authority and likely threshold:**
  - Direct purchase authority typically $5K–$100K depending on jurisdiction
  - MBE/DBE/SBE set-aside thresholds vary by state — often $50K–$250K
  - Term contracts and on-call vendor pools
- **Primary pain points:**
  - State and local set-aside utilization goals (MBE, DBE, SBE, veteran-owned) that are not being hit
  - Vendors who certify once and disappear
  - Limited certified vendor pools in pest control, janitorial, translation
  - Emergency mobilizations (storms, displacement, building closures) with no on-call vendor
  - Contract compliance reporting that requires real documentation
- **What they need to believe before contacting ARCG:**
  - ARCG holds the specific state/local certifications they recognize (MBE, SBE, DBE)
  - ARCG can be added to an on-call or term vendor pool quickly
  - ARCG won't ghost after the first contract
  - Pricing is reasonable and inside their threshold
- **Proof / trust signals that matter:**
  - State-level MBE / DBE / SBE certification numbers, current
  - HUBZone status if applicable to the jurisdiction
  - Local presence, local POC, local response
  - Clear service-lane breakdown with NAICS / commodity codes
  - Insurance and bonding posture stated plainly (or "bonding via verified sub")
- **Objections:**
  - "We already have vendors for that" — handled with set-aside compliance angle and bench-vendor positioning
  - "What's your local footprint?" — handled with mobilization language
  - "Are you licensed for pest control in our state?" — handled honestly; if not, scope is limited to lanes ARCG can self-perform or sub
- **Preferred outreach angle:**
  - Set-aside utilization framing, on-call vendor pool framing, emergency-mobilization framing
  - LinkedIn + direct email to MWBE coordinator
- **Best content topics:**
  - State set-aside compliance basics
  - On-call vendor pools — what gets you added
  - Emergency mobilization for facility events
  - Translation services for community health and public services
  - Janitorial post-event cleanup
- **CTA that fits the buyer:**
  - "Add ARCG to your vendor pool"
  - "Request a quote"
  - "Schedule a facilities support discussion"
- **Offers most relevant to them:**
  - On-call vendor agreement template-ready
  - Multi-lane bundled response (pest + janitorial + translation)
  - Emergency response standby posture
  - Translation & interpretation for community-facing programs

## Avatar 3 — Prime Contractor PM

- **Role / title:** Program Manager, Subcontracts Manager, Capture Manager, Small Business Liaison Officer (SBLO), Teaming Lead
- **Agency or organization type:** Large primes and mid-tier primes holding federal IDIQs, BPAs, MATOC, GSA Schedules — facility services, base operations, and integrated services primes
- **Buying authority and likely threshold:**
  - Subcontract awards from $25K to $1M+
  - Required to report subcontracting plan compliance (SDVOSB 3%, SB 23%, etc.)
  - Capture-stage teaming agreements where ARCG fills a set-aside gap
- **Primary pain points:**
  - Subcontracting plan goals are being missed; auditors are asking
  - Need socioeconomic subs who can actually self-perform a defined scope
  - Subs who can't produce insurance certs, W-9s, or bonding documentation
  - Subs who disappear during performance
  - Teaming agreements that fall through at proposal time
- **What they need to believe before contacting ARCG:**
  - ARCG's SDVOSB / MBE / DBE / SBE / HUBZone certifications are current and verifiable
  - ARCG can produce a clean reps-and-certs package fast
  - ARCG will sign a teaming agreement and stay engaged through proposal and performance
  - ARCG self-performs a defined scope and doesn't just pass through
- **Proof / trust signals that matter:**
  - SAM.gov record, SBA VetCert status
  - Insurance posture (GL minimum, willing to add additional insured)
  - Past performance summary at the lane level
  - Single POC who responds inside 24 hours during capture
- **Objections:**
  - "We need a sub who can actually perform" — handled with prime-managed, sub-supported delivery language
  - "Can you commit by proposal deadline?" — handled with 24–72 hour response SLA
  - "Are your certs really active?" — handled with verifiable cert references
- **Preferred outreach angle:**
  - Teaming-first, subcontracting-plan-compliance framing
  - LinkedIn message to SBLO or Subcontracts Manager with one-page teaming sheet
- **Best content topics:**
  - SDVOSB subcontracting compliance for primes
  - Teaming agreement essentials
  - Set-aside socioeconomic gap-fills
  - Vendor documentation primes actually need
  - Facility support lane coverage for primes
- **CTA that fits the buyer:**
  - "Open a teaming discussion"
  - "Request our cert package"
  - "Schedule a capabilities call"
- **Offers most relevant to them:**
  - Teaming agreement-ready posture
  - Sub-tier execution under prime
  - Set-aside compliance contribution
  - Documentation package ready in 24 hours

## Avatar 4 — Facilities Director

- **Role / title:** Facilities Director, Director of Operations, Building Services Manager, Property Manager (institutional), Maintenance Director
- **Agency or organization type:** Federal buildings, state/local government buildings, public schools, universities, hospitals (non-clinical facility ops), public housing, museums, courthouses, correctional facilities, transit hubs
- **Buying authority and likely threshold:**
  - Direct facility services purchases typically $1K–$50K per engagement
  - Annual contract authority for recurring services often $25K–$250K
  - Emergency authority for after-hours response
- **Primary pain points:**
  - Pest sightings during occupancy; complaints from tenants/staff
  - Janitorial gaps after events, incidents, or staff turnover
  - Translation gaps during community-facing programs
  - Staffing gaps during turnover, peak season, or events
  - Emergency cleanup and standup after storm, flood, or displacement
  - Vendors who don't show up
- **What they need to believe before contacting ARCG:**
  - ARCG can mobilize quickly — same-day to 72 hours depending on lane
  - Service is eco-friendly, ADA-compliant, safety-aware
  - Insurance and licensing posture is in order
  - One vendor can handle multiple lanes (pest + janitorial + staffing + emergency)
- **Proof / trust signals that matter:**
  - Plain language about response times
  - Photos of service-ready posture (vehicles, uniformed crew, equipment)
  - Eco-friendly / IPM language for pest control
  - ADA-compliant service language
  - Local POC, real phone number
- **Objections:**
  - "We have a vendor already" — handled as backup / overflow / emergency posture
  - "How fast can you actually show up?" — handled with stated response window per lane
  - "Are you licensed for pest control here?" — handled honestly; lanes are scoped to what ARCG can deliver
- **Preferred outreach angle:**
  - Operational, calm, problem-solving tone
  - Facebook and Instagram for visibility, LinkedIn for the procurement conversation
  - Direct email or call with a one-page service sheet
- **Best content topics:**
  - Pest control + IPM best practices for public buildings
  - Janitorial readiness for events and incidents
  - Emergency mobilization framed safely (no client names)
  - Translation for community-facing facilities
  - Staffing gap coverage
- **CTA that fits the buyer:**
  - "Request a facilities support quote"
  - "Schedule a pest control walkthrough"
  - "Request emergency response standby info"
- **Offers most relevant to them:**
  - Pest control & extermination (NAICS 561710)
  - Janitorial & facility support
  - Translation & interpretation
  - Staffing & operational support
  - Emergency & relief services standby
